About OB-BIA LATAM

The Latin American Bioimpedance Observatory (OB-BIA LATAM) is a multinational scientific network dedicated to the study, validation, and dissemination of the ANI BIS-E advanced nutritional assessment system (Comprehensive Nutritional Analysis by Bioimpedance Spectroscopy).

Formally established through Charter No. 001-2026, the observatory integrates assessments from Colombia, Mexico, Argentina, and Brazil, with a standardized protocol that captures 259 variables per subject evaluated, including 8 advanced bioelectrical indicators.

Our mission is to generate Latin American scientific evidence on body composition and bioelectrical indicators in diverse populations, contributing to the development of reference values specific to Latin America.

🔬 Clinical research 📊 Nutritional epidemiology ⚡ Vector bioimpedance
🎯 Mission

Generate, systematize, and disseminate scientific knowledge about body composition in Latin American populations through the standardized application of the ANI BIS-E system.

🔭 Vision

To be the Ibero-American benchmark in applied advanced bioimpedance research, with proprietary normative values and validated protocols for the populations of Latin America.

📐 Methodology

Standardized ANI BIS-E protocol with 259 variables per subject. Includes 8 advanced bioelectrical indicators, multidomain assessment, and MCCB body composition classification.

Assessment system

Indicators ANI BIS-E

Comprehensive system of 8 bioelectrical metrics derived from bioimpedance spectroscopy analysis, designed to assess cellular function, fluid balance, and metabolic adaptation in advanced nutritional assessment.

⚡
IFC
Cellular Function Index
IFC = Φ° / Φ°ref
Φ°ref ♂ = 7.5°  |  ♀ = 6.5°

Reflects the integrity and functionality of the cell membrane. Values ≥ 1.0 indicate optimal cellular function. Directly correlates with active body cell mass.

Cellular function
🔄
IRC
Cellular Resistance Index
IRC = Rp / Rd
Rp peripheral  |  Rd dielectric

Assesses segment-normalized bioelectrical resistance. Deviations from the reference range indicate hydro-cellular dysfunction or compartment imbalance.

Cellular hydration
φ
PABU
Golden Pattern of Ultracellular Balance
PABU = ECW / ICW
Optimal: PABU → φ = 1.618

Normalized ratio between extracellular and intracellular water. The optimal value converges toward the golden ratio (φ = 1.618), an indicator of perfect cellular homeostasis and maximum bioelectrical coherence.

Golden ratio · φ
Δφ
ICA BIS
BIS Harmonic Coherence Index
ICA BIS = |PABU − 1.618|
Optimal: ICA BIS → 0

Absolute distance between the observed PABU and the golden ratio. The closer to zero, the greater the bioelectrical coherence. Discriminates between states of homeostasis and cellular imbalance.

Bioelectrical coherence
⚙️
ISCM
Mitochondrial Cellular Synthesis Index
ISCM = MCC / (FFM × 0.45)
MCC = Body Cell Mass

Reflects the capacity for mitochondria-mediated cellular synthesis and renewal. Correlates with the functional quality of the active cellular compartment and the state of biological vitality.

Cellular metabolism
💧
IEHH
Hydro-Homeostatic Efficiency Index
IEHH = ACT / (0.73 × FFM)
Ref: IEHH ∈ [0.92, 1.08]

Assesses the efficiency of the body's fluid regulation system. The hydration fraction of fat-free mass is a sensitive predictor of nutritional status and renal function.

Fluid balance
⚖️
EB-BIS
BIS Bioelectrical Balance
EB-BIS = ECW / TBW
Ref ♂: 0.39–0.42  |  ♀: 0.40–0.43

Ratio between extracellular water and total body water. Deviations indicate overhydration, dehydration, or systemic inflammation. Relevant in clinical and sports monitoring.

Fluid compartments
🧬
IAE
Environmental Adaptation Index
IAE = (Xc / R) × (1000 / height_cm)
Normalized by segment and height

Relates height-normalized reactance and resistance, estimating the body's bioelectrical adaptation capacity to stimuli from the metabolic and nutritional environment.

Metabolic adaptation
