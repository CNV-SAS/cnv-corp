About OB-BIA LATAM

The Latin American Bioimpedance Observatory (OB-BIA LATAM) is a multinational scientific network dedicated to the study, validation, and dissemination of the ANI BIS-E care model (a health, food, and nutrition care model informed by bioimpedance spectroscopy and epigenetics).

Formally established through Charter No. 001-2026, the observatory integrates assessments from Colombia, Mexico, Argentina, and Brazil, with a standardized protocol that captures 259 variables per subject evaluated, including 8 advanced bioelectrical indicators.

Our mission is to generate Latin American scientific evidence on bioelectrical health in diverse populations, contributing to the development of reference values specific to Latin America.

🔬 Clinical research 📊 Nutritional epidemiology ⚡ Bioimpedance spectroscopy
🎯 Mission

Generate, systematize, and disseminate scientific knowledge about bioelectrical health in Latin American populations through the application of the ANI BIS-E model.

🔭 Vision

To be the Latin American benchmark in research on the molecular bioelectrical health of Latin American populations.

📐 Methodology

ANI BIS-E care model with 259 variables per subject. Includes 8 bioelectrical indicators derived from the Cole-Cole model, multidomain assessment, and the map of Structure (FMI vs FFMI), Function (IFC), and Risk (IRC) BIS (EFyR BIS).

Indicator system

Indicators ANI BIS-E

Comprehensive system of 8 bioelectrical metrics derived from bioimpedance spectroscopy analysis, designed to assess cellular function, fluid balance, and metabolic adaptation within the ANI BIS-E care model.

⚡
IFC
Cell Function Index
IFC = (C / R∞) × 1000
Risk: ♂ < 3.8  |  ♀ < 2.2

An evolution of the phase angle built from the primary properties of the Cole-Cole model: membrane capacitance (C) and infinite-frequency resistance (R∞). It reflects active cell function and mass, and is stable with age. A high IFC indicates better cell function.

Cell function
🔄
IRC
Cell Risk Index
IRC = Re / (Ri × C) × 10
High risk: ♂ > 2.1  |  ♀ > 2.8

Built from extracellular resistance (Re), intracellular resistance (Ri), and membrane capacitance (C). It flags low-grade inflammation and membrane damage —the state of the cellular environment— independently of age. A high IRC indicates greater risk.

Cell risk
φ
PABU
Uribe Bioelectrical Golden Ratio
PABU = [(Re + Ri) × k] / (R∞ × C)
k ♂ = 0.78  |  ♀ = 0.46  ·  Optimal → φ = 1.618

Integrates the four Cole-Cole model parameters (Re, Ri, R∞, C) with a sex-recalibrated constant k. It converges toward the golden number (φ = 1.618) in cellular homeostasis; its deviation is a graded marker of structural impairment: deficit raises it above φ, while excess adiposity lowers it.

Golden ratio · φ
Δφ
ICA BIS
Bioelectrical Allostatic Load Index
ICA-BIS = PABU − φ
φ = 1.618  ·  Optimal → 0

The PABU expressed as a signed distance from the golden number. The sign indicates the direction of the allostatic load: positive for structural deficit, negative for excess adiposity. Inherits the molecular level of the PABU.

Allostatic load
⚙️
ISCM
Cardiometabolic Susceptibility Index
ISCM-BIS = z(MCA) + z(AIC) + z(E/I) + z(FFMI) + z(ACT)

An additive model of five bioelectrical z-scores: active cell mass, intracellular water, extra/intracellular ratio, fat-free mass index, and total body water. It integrates cardiometabolic susceptibility. Categories: low ≤ −1, mild −1 to 1, moderate 1 to 2.5, high > 2.5.

Cardiometabolic susceptibility
💧
IEHH
Human Hydration Spectrum Index
IEHH = 0.25·z(Re/R∞) + 0.25·z(FFW) + 0.50·z(C)

Combines three weighted bioelectrical z-scores —the Re/R∞ ratio, fat-free water (FFW), and membrane capacitance (C)— to describe the cellular hydration spectrum. Categories: optimal ≤ 0, mild 0 to 1, moderate 1 to 2, high > 2.

Cellular hydration
⚖️
EB-BIS
Bioelectrical Age
EB-BIS = estimated biological age
Ridge regression on Cole-Cole parameters (v3)

Estimates biological age from the bioelectrical parameters of the Cole-Cole model, without including chronological age in the formula (avoiding circularity). Comparing it with actual age reveals cellular aging.

Biological age
🧬
IAE
Aging Acceleration Index
IAE = EB-BIS − Age
Adjusted by residualization against age

The difference between bioelectrical and chronological age, adjusted to be independent of age. It measures the pace of aging relative to peers of the same age: accelerated, concordant, or decelerated.

Aging pace
